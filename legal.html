Moto Mission

Privacy, terms of use, and motorcycle safety information for the pre-release Moto Mission engineering and ride platform.

PrivacyTermsSafetyYour controls

Experimental software

Moto Mission is not a vehicle controller, certified navigation system, emergency-response service, or substitute for road signs, service manuals, inspections, professional advice, or rider judgment. Do not interact with handheld controls while operating a motorcycle.

Privacy policy

Depending on the features you enable, Moto Mission may process account information, motorcycle profiles, maintenance and engineering records, precise or approximate location, GPS tracks, speed, heading, trip distance, motion and telemetry, photos, video, audio, notes, uploaded files, diagnostics, device information, and provider-usage records.

This information is used to authenticate users, record and display rides, synchronize data, maintain motorcycle and engineering records, provide user-selected ride features, protect accounts, investigate failures, enforce provider limits, and improve reliability.

Moto Mission does not sell personal information and must not use ride or location information for targeted advertising. Precise routes are private by default and should be accessible only to the authenticated owner unless deliberately shared.

Service providers

The service may use Apple, Vercel, Supabase, mapping or road-data providers, and other infrastructure vendors. Information required to operate a selected feature may be sent to those providers under their respective terms and privacy practices. Privileged provider credentials remain server-side.

Retention

Data is retained only as needed to provide the pre-release service, protect it, maintain backups, meet legal obligations, or preserve records requested by the user. Final production retention periods and account-deletion timelines will be published before broad distribution.

Your controls

Optional permissions should be requested only when their related feature is used. Denying optional location, camera, microphone, motion, Bluetooth, photo, or notification permission should leave a reduced but usable experience.

Before broader release, users must be able to review account data, stop ride recording, delete rides and uploads, export ride and maintenance records, revoke sessions, and request account deletion.

Terms of use

Moto Mission is provided as experimental pre-release software. Features may change, fail, provide incomplete information, or be removed. Access may be limited or suspended to protect users, infrastructure, or the project.

Users must protect credentials and may not access another person's account or data, bypass security controls or provider limits, upload malicious or unlawful content, interfere with service availability, or use Moto Mission to track, threaten, or endanger another person.

Users retain ownership of their submitted content and grant only the limited permission needed to host, process, display, back up, and transmit it to provide requested features.

To the maximum extent allowed by law, the service is provided “as is” and “as available,” without guarantees of accuracy, uninterrupted operation, or fitness for a particular purpose. Applicable law may limit these terms.

Motorcycle safety

  • Road signs, traffic laws, official instructions, and rider judgment are authoritative.
  • Speed limits, road details, fuel availability, weather, lean, pitch, maintenance predictions, and alerts may be delayed, estimated, incomplete, or wrong.
  • Loss of the phone, cloud, GPS, Bluetooth, cellular service, or Moto Mission must not affect normal motorcycle operation.
  • Emergency-related features cannot guarantee delivery and do not replace emergency services.
  • Stop safely before changing settings or reviewing detailed information.

Security and contact

Suspected vulnerabilities, unauthorized access, or privacy concerns should be reported privately to the project administrator through the verified invitation or testing channel. Do not post credentials, exploit details, precise routes, or another user's information publicly.

These policies are a pre-release engineering baseline and require review against final product behavior and applicable law before commercial or broad public launch.
