# Moto Mission Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** July 16, 2026

Moto Mission is a pre-release motorcycle engineering, maintenance, and ride-logging platform. This policy describes the categories of information the service may process, why it is used, and the controls expected before broader distribution.

This baseline must be reviewed against the final production behavior and by qualified counsel before a public commercial launch.

## Information processed

Depending on enabled features, Moto Mission may process:

- account information, including email address, display name, role, and authentication records
- motorcycle profiles, parts, maintenance records, engineering notes, tasks, and uploaded files
- precise or approximate location, GPS tracks, heading, speed, trip distance, and ride timestamps
- motion and telemetry data, including acceleration, orientation, lean or pitch estimates, and device or vehicle sensor readings
- photos, videos, audio recordings, notes, and other user-provided content
- device, browser, app version, diagnostic, security, and network information
- provider usage records needed to enforce API limits and prevent abuse

Moto Mission does not sell personal information and must not use ride or location data for targeted advertising.

## Purposes

Information may be used to:

- provide authentication and account access
- record, display, analyze, export, and synchronize rides
- maintain motorcycle service and engineering records
- provide safety-related alerts and user-selected ride displays
- operate cloud storage, mapping, road, weather, or navigation-related features
- detect abuse, protect accounts, investigate faults, and improve reliability
- comply with legal obligations and enforce applicable terms

## Location and riding data

Precise location and ride history can reveal sensitive habits, routes, homes, workplaces, and regularly visited places. Moto Mission must:

- request location permission only when needed
- explain foreground and background location use before collection
- provide a visible recording state
- allow ride recording to be stopped
- avoid public sharing by default
- restrict access to the authenticated owner unless the user deliberately shares data
- provide deletion and export controls before broader distribution

Speed limits, road details, alerts, and derived riding metrics may be incomplete, delayed, estimated, or incorrect. They are not a substitute for road signs, rider judgment, or applicable law.

## Third-party services

Moto Mission may use service providers such as Apple, Vercel, Supabase, map or road-data providers, and other infrastructure vendors. These providers process information under their own terms and privacy practices. Provider keys and privileged credentials must remain server-side.

The final public policy must list the production providers actually enabled and the categories of data sent to each.

## Retention

Information should be retained only as long as needed for the service, security, legal obligations, backups, or user-requested records. Production must define retention periods for:

- ride GPS and telemetry
- uploads and media
- authentication and security logs
- deleted-account backups
- provider usage and billing records

## User controls

Before external release, users must be able to:

- review and correct account and motorcycle information
- export ride and maintenance data in a usable format
- delete individual rides and uploads
- request account deletion
- revoke device sessions
- disable optional location, analytics, and notification features

## Security

Moto Mission uses technical and organizational safeguards intended to protect information, including authenticated access, database authorization policies, encrypted transport, secret separation, restricted storage, logging, and release review. No system is completely secure.

## Children

Moto Mission is not designed for children under 13 and should not knowingly collect their personal information. Broader release may require higher age limits depending on location and intended use.

## Changes

Material policy changes should be dated, summarized, and presented to users before newly expanded collection or use begins.

## Contact

A monitored privacy and security contact method must be published before inviting external users.
